Why we made the application

The two main problems in current road trip applications which the Journey application intends to solve are the inability of current applications to coordinate destinations across multiple cars and that current applications only search for POIs around your current location rather than along your current route.

How it is different from other navigation applications

A problem that exists in current solutions comes down to the lack of a “caravan” feature. The “caravan” feature would allow users to coordinate road trips with other cars so that everyone in the group would have the same destination as well as sharing the location and route of all members of the “caravan”. Users need this feature because it is easy for drivers to lose people who exit to get food while simply following navigation. By creating a “group” for each road trip we believe that sharing one common “caravan destination” with everyone in the group will solve this problem.

Journey is Better

When using navigation in today’s solutions, searching for places while navigating provides places that are ranked based on absolute distance to the user’s current location. While on a road trip, users would like to be able to find places to stop at along their programmed route, such as a food or gas stop. A user on a road trip would like to filter potential stopping points based on distance off their route and time along their route. For example, the feature that is lacking in existing solutions is the ability to ask for a list of all restaurants one hour to two hours down the road and within 2 miles of my current route. This functionality would be very useful for a car on a road trip who is wishing to plan out some sort of stop for sometime in the future
